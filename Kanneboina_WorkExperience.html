Work Experience

Field Trips

SAP

As I am currently in the Dublin High School Engineering Academy, I have been able to go to many field trips to engineering companies and listen to experienced guest speakers. During my freshman year, I went on a field trip to SAP. Here, I was able to talk to employees of SAP from different departments. Through this trip, I understood that one of the most integral parts of working in a team is being able to efficiently convey information to people of different experience levels. For example, oftentimes, a software engineer must communicate with an employee in finance in order to understand design constraints.

NASA Ames Research Center

One field trip that I have attended through the Dublin High School Engineering Program is NASA Ames Research Center. This was a very insightful experience because it taught me the different areas of aerospace engineering. For example, I was able to look at an old rocket from the inside and understand how it worked. The tour guides also explain how they prototyped and designed the rocket as well as what factors they took into account when building the rocket. I learned that when designing and manufacturing the rocket, they have to take into account the cost efficiency, effects on the environment, durability, and functionality. Another interesting thing I saw when I visited NASA Ames was the robots that they were building. This was interesting for many reasons, one being that I never knew saw a professional robotics lab before coming to NASA Ames. This was very impactful for me because I am currently on a robotics team. We were able to meet one of the designers for a robot that utilized artificial intelligence. He explained that the purpose of this robot was to explore outer space terrains. What made this robot special was that it was able to assess its own battery level and could determine whether it should start heading back to charge itself or if it had enough battery to keep exploring. I was very intrigued by this robot because not only did it require good build quality to withstand unknown terrains, but it also required complex coding. This was intriguing to me because it combined two of my favorite things in engineering which are coding and building. Through this experience , I was able to gain more knowledge about the aerospace engineering field and the different types of jobs offered at NASA Ames.

UC Davis

In hopes of learning more about the college application process and more about my future in college, I went on a field trip to UC Davis. Here, I learned more about their research programs, academic courses, and student body. Learning about their engineering research and programs was one of the factors that helped me decide to major in biomedical engineering.

Lawrance Livermore National Laboratories

Another field trip that interested me was when I went to Lawrance Livermore National Laboratories(LLNL) during my junior year. When I took honors chemistry during my sophomore year, I learned about fusion. Since I was interested in learning more about how exactly fusion is created, I signed up for this field trip during my junior year. During this field trip, I was able to go on a tour around the Lawrance Livermore National Laboratories’ office campus. In this tour, I learned a lot about how LLNL is trying to attain fusion in order to have more efficient electricity-based technology. Although the beams of light are only shot for a few seconds, employees at LLNL work for hours and put a lot of money in order to build the pathway to guide the beams that have to hit a very small target. Through this field trip, I understood how much work is put into trying to create fusion.

Roche Molecular

Another field trip that I participated in was one that Roche Molecular hosted. This field trip allowed me to explore the field of biomedical engineering. I learned about how this company manufactured assays, which are used to measure the amount of substance present in a biological or chemical process. I also learned how technology is used to speed up testing and manufacturing processes.

Guest Speakers


During my time in the engineering program, I have met a number of industry professionals through workplace field trips and guest speakers. One of the guest speakers that came into my Principles of Engineering class was Aaron Pomerantz. Mr.Pomerantz was a bioengineer that traveled to many places including the Amazon rainforest so that he could study organisms ranging from insects to mammals. One of the devices that he was able to bioengineer was a portable microscope. He explained that this was very useful for bioengineers when they are conducting studies because many bioengineers are not in a lab when they are working. They are often outdoors, which makes it harder to use bulky microscopes to observe their specimen. Another device that he bioengineered is a portable gene sequencer. He explained how this would help bioengineers quickly obtain useful information. This guest speaker was very eye-opening to me because before this, I was not aware that biology and engineering could be so closely integrated. This guest speaker was impactful to me because it made me consider to be a bioengineer in the future as I was very interested in the things that Mr.Pomerantz was able to do in his job as a bioengineer. Another guest speaker that I enjoyed was Shawn Mehaffey, who presented in my Advanced Placement Computer Science Applications class. Mr.Mehaffey was a systems analyst who worked in Chevron. When he was presenting to my class, he talked about the evolution of technology and how machines today have developed over time. He also explained how the technology worked twenty years ago. Listening to his presentation helped me learn where the computers we use today originated from. This sparked my interest in learning about the material and work that goes into engineering a computer and how far technology today has come. He also talked to us about his pathway in Chevron by explaining how he was first hired and eventually was able to work as a systems analyst in Chevron. This helped me understand more about how an engineer can advance to higher positions in a company such as Chevron.
